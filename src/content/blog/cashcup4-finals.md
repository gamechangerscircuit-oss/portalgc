FOKUS Sakura reinforced their position as one of the strongest teams in the European Game Changers tier-two scene after defeating Bitfix Puppies 2–1 to win Cash Cup #4.

The Grand Final delivered a highly competitive series between two teams whose rivalry has continued to grow throughout the season. FOKUS opened the match with a dominant 13–5 victory on Ascent before Bitfix Puppies responded with a 13–10 win on Pearl, forcing the series onto Fracture. There, FOKUS Sakura closed out the tournament and secure the title.

## A rivalry built through promotion battles

The rivalry between FOKUS Sakura and Bitfix Puppies was built during the Stage 1 Promotion/Relegation tournament, where both teams repeatedly crossed paths throughout one of the most competitive qualification events of the VALORANT EMEA Game Changers season.

The event ultimately ended with FOKUS Sakura securing qualification for Stage 2, while Joblife GC were left outside the main event. Months later, FOKUS entered Cash Cup #4 as the tournament’s only active Stage 2 team, immediately positioning them as the clear favorite across the bracket.

Meanwhile, Bitfix Puppies arrived as one of the strongest challengers outside the established top tier, turning the Cash Cup #4 Grand Final into another chapter in a rivalry already defined by elimination series, adaptation battles, and promotion pressure.

## FOKUS Sakura dominate the bracket

FOKUS Sakura produced the cleanest run of the tournament, reaching the Grand Final without dropping a single map. Their path to the title included convincing victories over Team Réality, WLG Female Stars, and ORA Esport GC, while consistently controlling the pace of matches from the opening rounds onward.

Across the bracket, FOKUS relied on disciplined defensive setups, efficient utility coordination, and strong late-round structure to systematically shut down opponents before momentum could fully develop.

That level of control ultimately became one of the defining characteristics of their tournament run and proved decisive once again in the Grand Final against Bitfix Puppies.

## Bitfix Puppies survive the tougher road

Unlike FOKUS’ controlled path through the playoffs, Bitfix Puppies reached the Grand Final after navigating a far more demanding bracket.

Victories over FG Dames and Habos Babos secured their early progression, but Caldya Esport GC pushed Bitfix to the limit in the semifinals, forcing a deciding third map before the roster eventually secured qualification for the final.

Across the tournament the roster repeatedly improved throughout longer series, adjusting tempo and mid-round decision-making whenever opponents managed to gain momentum.

## Europe’s challengers continue to improve

Beyond the two finalists, Cash Cup #4 also highlighted the increasing depth of the European Game Changers tier-two scene.

ORA Esport GC reached the semifinals after one of the most convincing undefeated runs in the bracket, but ultimately struggled to break through FOKUS Sakura’s disciplined defensive structure.

Meanwhile, Caldya Esport GC reinforced their status as a dangerous emerging roster after pushing Bitfix Puppies to three maps in one of the closest playoff series of the event.

While neither team reached the Grand Final, both organizations showed that the gap between Europe’s established contenders and its emerging challengers continues to shrink.
